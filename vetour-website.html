Our Story

Uganda's First Liberation Heritage Tourism Company

VETOUR (U) Ltd was founded with a singular mission: to transform Uganda's rich liberation history into living, breathing tourism experiences. We believe that history is not confined to textbooks — it walks among us in the stories of veterans, the stones of heritage sites, and the cultural memory of our people.

We curate immersive journeys that connect visitors with the authentic narrative of Uganda's journey from conflict to resilience, offering encounters with the very people who shaped the nation's destiny.

The Liberation Trail

Uganda's Journey Through History

Walk the milestones of Uganda's liberation — from turbulence to triumph, told through the places and people that lived it.

1962
The Dawn of Independence
Uganda achieves independence from British colonial rule. The moment that changed everything — visit Kampala's independence monument and relive the euphoria.
1971 – 1979
The Dark Years
The Idi Amin era — a period of immense suffering and resistance. Our tours explore the memorials, testimonies, and the enduring spirit of survival.
1981 – 1986
The Liberation Struggle
The National Resistance Movement's guerrilla campaign for liberation. Walk the Luwero Triangle — ground zero of Uganda's modern liberation story.
1986
Liberation Day
The NRM captures Kampala. A new chapter begins. Experience the sites of liberation, the speeches, the transformation — told by those who were present.
Present Day
Living Heritage
Uganda today — a nation honouring its past while building its future. VETOUR bridges then and now through immersive heritage tourism experiences.
